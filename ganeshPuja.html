Celebration of Ganesh Puja in Cuttack

Ganesh Puja in Cuttack is celebrated with immense devotion and grandeur, marking the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity known as the remover of obstacles and the god of wisdom and prosperity. The festival usually spans ten days, culminating in the grand immersion ceremony (Ganesh Visarjan) on Anant Chaturdashi. During this period, beautifully crafted idols of Lord Ganesha are installed in homes and public pandals (temporary structures), adorned with flowers, lights, and intricate decorations. Devotees engage in various rituals, including chanting mantras, singing bhajans (devotional songs), and offering sweets like modaks, which are believed to be Lord Ganesha's favorite. The atmosphere is filled with joy, music, and dance as communities come together to celebrate this auspicious occasion.

Famous Ganesh Puja Places in Cuttack

Cuttack hosts some of the most spectacular Ganesh Puja celebrations in Odisha, with numerous locations becoming pilgrimage sites during the festival. These places attract thousands of devotees who come to seek blessings and witness the grandeur of the celebrations.

This is one of the oldest and most iconic hubs for Ganesh Puja in Cuttack. The pandals here are known for their grand size, artistic themes, and intricate decorations made by skilled artisans. During the festival, the entire area turns into a fairground with stalls selling food, toys, and puja items. The vibrant lighting and music draw huge crowds every evening, making it a must-visit spot.
