History OF Cuttack

Cuttack, one of the oldest cities in eastern India, was founded in 989 CE by King Nrupa Keshari and served as a strategic military base, with the Barabati Fort at its heart. Over the centuries, it grew into a prominent administrative and cultural center under various dynasties including the Somavamshis, Eastern Gangas, and later the Mughals and Marathas. Its location at the confluence of the Mahanadi and Kathajodi rivers made it a vital center for trade, governance, and defense. The city flourished culturally and architecturally, with temples, mosques, and forts reflecting its diverse history.


Under British rule, Cuttack became the capital of Odisha and saw major developments in education, law, and infrastructure, including the founding of Ravenshaw College and the establishment of legal institutions. Though the capital shifted to Bhubaneswar in 1948, Cuttack remains the judicial capital of Odisha and an important cultural hub. It is famous for its silver filigree work (Tarakasi), the annual Bali Jatra festival, and as the birthplace of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose.

Festivals

Durga Puja

Cuttack's Durga Puja is legendary, featuring some of the most artistic and innovative pandals in India. The city comes alive with festivities, cultural programs, and the famous silver decorations that make our celebration unique.

Makar Sankranti

The Makar Sankranti celebrations in Cuttack are marked by beautiful decorations, traditional rituals, and community gatherings. The festival showcases the deep spiritual connection of our people with the divine mother.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi in Cuttack is celebrated with great fervor, featuring elaborate decorations, cultural programs, and community feasts. The festival brings people together to honor Lord Ganesha and seek his blessings.

Tourism

Barabati Fort

Barabati Fort is a 14th-century fort in Cuttack built by the Eastern Ganga dynasty, now known for its ancient ramparts, moat, and stone gateway that showcase the cityâ€™s medieval heritage.

Netaji Birth Place

Visit the birthplace of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, now converted into a museum showcasing his life and India's freedom struggle. This heritage site attracts visitors from across the globe.

Dhabaleswar Temple

Located on a scenic island in the Mahanadi River, this ancient Shiva temple is accessible by a suspension bridge and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Chandini Chowk Market

Experience the vibrant local culture at Chandini Chowk, famous for its silver jewelry, traditional textiles, and local handicrafts. It's a shopper's paradise in the heart of the city.

Mahanadi Riverfront

Enjoy peaceful moments along the Mahanadi riverbank, perfect for evening walks, boat rides, and witnessing beautiful sunsets. The river is the lifeline of our city.

Ansupa Lake

Ansupa Lake is Odisha's largest freshwater lake, famous for its scenic beauty, migratory birds, boating, and lush greenery. A tranquil spot for nature lovers and photographers.
